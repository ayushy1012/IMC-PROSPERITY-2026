# Round 2 - Advisor Insights (Orin's Log)

*These insights are provided by Orin, the On-Board Advisor, regarding the trading mechanics, Market Access Fee, and competitive strategies for Round 2.*

## Opportunity Valuation
Valuation is not just about what something is worth to the market, but what it is worth to *you*.

**Key Takeaways for the Market Access Fee (MAF):**
*   **The Threshold of Opportunity:** The Market Access Fee is a threshold that dictates when an opportunity stops being profitable. It is not just a number to subtract from your profit.
*   **Cost vs. Advantage:** At what point does the cost of market access eat up the advantage the additional 25% volume was supposed to create?
*   **Reverse Engineering:** Work backwards. Start with your desired outcome, ask what the access can cost *at most* to make it worthwhile, and compare that to the actual cost. The answer lies in that gap.
*   **Don't Overpay:** The margin can disappear if you pay too much for access just to chase upside.

## Competing With Others
Winning full market access is not just about your math; it's about predicting everyone else's math.

**Key Takeaways for the Blind Auction:**
*   **Target the Median:** To win full market access, you need to outbid the **median**, not the highest bidder. You are trying to read the middle of the pack and land just above it.
*   **Market Psychology:** How do other traders value this advantage? A cautious field means the median sits lower; an ambitious field pushes it up.
*   **The Sweet Spot:** Underestimating the field means you get locked out. Overestimating means you pay more than the access was worth. The goal is to find the sweet spot between those two extremes.

## Risk Appetite
Winning is not the same as winning well. Securing what you wanted while paying too much is a hollow victory.

**Key Takeaways for Bidding Efficiency:**
*   **The Median is the Threshold, Not the Ceiling:** Because you only need to finish in the top 50%, the median is the line you must cross.
*   **Efficiency vs. Waste:** Bidding very high guarantees access, but could you have achieved the same result for fewer XIRECs? Extra spending is often waste dressed up as caution.
*   **Efficiency vs. Risk:** The real task is finding how low you can place your bid while still being reasonably sure you land above the median. The closer you get to that threshold, the more efficient your spend becomes.
*   **The Danger of the Edge:** However, if you bid too close to the edge and the field is slightly more aggressive than anticipated, you risk being locked out entirely.
